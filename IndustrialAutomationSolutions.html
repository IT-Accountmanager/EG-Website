INDUSTRIAL AUTOMATION SOLUTIONS

             

        
Envision specializes in BIW (Body-in-White) robotics simulation & our advanced simulation
 solutions are designed to optimize and streamline the automation processes in the
 automotive industry. Using advanced technologies and systems, mechanical engineers can
 automate and optimize industrial processes, increase productivity, and improve
 operational efficiency with industrial automation solutions. Industrial automation solutions
 typically integrate hardware, software, and control systems to streamline operations and
 reduce manual intervention. 
 Here are some of their key features:
            

            
In industrial automation, robots are often used to perform repetitive or complex tasks with
 accuracy and efficiency. Advanced software tools are used to simulate & optimize the
 robotic processes involved in BIW assembly, such as welding, handling & material
 placement. Using state-of-the-art robotic simulation software, we virtually program and
 validate robot trajectories and sequences. This allows us to verify the feasibility and
 accuracy of the robot's movements, ensuring collision-free operations, optimizing cycle
 times, and improving overall productivity. Virtual programming minimizes the need for
 physical prototypes and enables quick iterations and adjustments.

IMPLEMENTATION


                    
Envision implements offline programming techniques, enabling the robot programs to be developed and tested in a
 virtual environment before deployment. This significantly reduces downtime during production, as the robot
 programs can be quickly and accurately loaded onto the actual robots, minimizing the need for manual programming
 on the shop floor.
It is a type of industrial computer that controls and monitors machinery and processes in real-time. PLCs can be
 programmed to perform specific tasks, coordinate operations, and communicate with other systems, which ensures
 efficient and reliable process control.

They are used to automate the control of various devices and equipment on
the factory floor. A human-machine interface (HMI) is a way for operators to
interact with automation systems using a graphical user interface (GUI).
Operators can monitor and control industrial processes, make adjustments,
and respond to alarms and anomalies with HMIs, which display real-time data,
control parameters, and provide visual feedback on equipment and processes.
The objective of industrial automation is to optimize and control industrial
processes so that desired outcomes can be achieved. Variables such as speed,
temperature, pressure, and quality are regulated using algorithms and control
strategies. By optimizing and controlling processes, resources are efficiently
utilized, product quality is improved, waste is reduced, and productivity is
increased.
Integrating automation systems with enterprise-level systems, such as
manufacturing execution systems (MES), enterprise resource planning systems
(ERPs), and data analytics platforms, is often an integral part of industrial
automation solutions. This integration enables data sharing,
process optimization, production planning, and improved coordination
between shop floor operations and business functions.
